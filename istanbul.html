HISTORY

Istanbul, formerly knows as Byzantium and Constantinople has a rich history spanning over 2,500 years.
Its has been a strategic location for various empires, including the Roman,
Byzantine, and Ottoman empires.

Another name

Istanbul was Byzantium (ancient Greek city) Constantinople (roman and byzantine capital)
Why famous Istanbul is famous for it
1. Rich history: blend of cultures, empires, and civilization
2. Architecture: mosque, churches, places, and bazaars.
3. Cultural heritage: vibrant arts, cuisine, and traditions.
4. Strategic location: bridge between Europe and Asia.

ANCIENT ERA( 660 BCE- 330 CE)

1-Byzantium: Founded by Greek colonists from megara in
660 BCE.
2-Roman Empire: Byzantium became a key city in the Roman Empire.

Byzantium ERA( 330 CE - -1453 CE )

1-Constantinople: Renamed by Empires constantine in
330 CE.
2-Eastern Roman Empire: Became the capital of the 2-Eastern
Roman Empire.
3-Golden Age: Reached its peek during the reign of justinianI.

OTTOMAN ERA( 1453 - 1922 CE)

1-Ottoman Conquest: MehmedII conquered Constantinople in
1453 CE.
2-Istanbul: Became the capital of the Ottoman Empire.
3-Growth and Development: Flourished under Ottoman rule.

Modern ERA( 1922CE - PRESENT)

Turkish Republic: Ankara became the new capital in
1923 CE.
Istanbul's Transformation: Continued to grow and modernize.

PLACES

Haggis Sophia:
A former byzantine church, Ottoman mosque, and now a museum,
showcasing Istanbul’s rich history
and architecture.

Blue Mosque:
An iconic mosque with six minarets, known for its stunning
blue tiles and beautiful architecture.

Galata Tower:
A medieval tower offering panoramic view of
Istanbul’s skyline.

Grand Baraz:
One of the oldest and largest covered markets in the world,
featuring a vast array of goods and souvenirs.

Basphorus Bridge:
A suspension bridge connecting Europe and Asia, offering
breathtaking views of Istanbul’s skyline.
